Week Overview

The first week of the project was held online and served as an introduction to the program, our teams, lecturers, and the different modules.

Main Goals

The week gave us an overview of Immersive Tech, UX Design, IoT, AI, and Data Science, and introduced us to the intercultural competencies we would need throughout the project.

  • Meet and get to know our team members and lecturers
  • Get a brief introduction to the project
  • Get a brief introduction to the modules and their focus areas
  • Start thinking about working in an international, cross-cultural setting

What I Learned

Even though it was only one week online, it gave me a chance to settle into the project and think about how I should approach things going forward.

I quickly realized that clear communication is key when working with a new team virtually, especially since we all come from different backgrounds and not everyone speaks English as a first language.

I also got a better sense of how each module fits into the bigger picture of the project, even if we only scratched the surface during the introductions.

Virtual Experience

Working entirely online presented unique challenges and opportunities for our international team.

We had to navigate different communication styles and technical setups while building the foundation for our collaboration.

The virtual format forced us to be more intentional about our communication and establish clear protocols from the beginning.

Team Dynamics

How our international team came together during the online week

Initial Connection

From the first online week, it honestly felt like we clicked right away, which was a nice surprise. The ice breaker project helped break the tension in our first meeting, and everyone was talkative and involved.

Communication felt pretty balanced, as no one tried to take charge, it was more equal, and we all had space to contribute. I saw myself as both a listener and a contributor, as I felt comfortable from the start.

Language & Communication

The only small challenge was language, sometimes complex English words made things tricky, but it never really slowed us down. What stood out the most was how quickly we got along and worked well as a team.

Being part of CyberTAG for the first week showed me how small things, like language differences or work styles, can change how a team functions. It made me realize the importance of clear communication, making sure everyone's on the same page, and being patient when someone struggles with a word or idea.

Communication Guidelines

Clear protocols for effective team collaboration

WhatsApp

For personal information
  • Quick updates and informal communication
  • Urgent matters requiring immediate attention
  • Team bonding and casual conversations

Email

For official documentation
  • Formal communication and official announcements
  • Document sharing and record keeping
  • Project updates and status reports

Teams

For team collaboration
  • Daily team communication and coordination
  • Quick questions and instant responses
  • Channel-based discussions by topic

GitHub

For code collaboration
  • Code reviews and pull requests
  • Issue tracking and bug reporting
  • Documentation and project wiki
