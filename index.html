About Me

Who am I?

My name is Mustapha Ismael Omar. I'd describe myself as someone who enjoys keeping things real and finding humour in just about any situation, usually the darker kind. Family is at the centre of my life; with four siblings, there's never a quiet moment, and I wouldn't have it any other way. I spend most of my free time working on our family farm or exploring the outdoors. I think I'm a mix between being social and keeping to myself, I enjoy going out with friends and family, but I also appreciate simple, quiet moments. Coffee and chocolate are my small daily joys, and I'm naturally adventurous. I like testing myself in new situations, and I've been told I can sometimes be a little too daring, taking risks without always thinking through the consequences, but I see that as part of what makes me adaptable, resourceful, and able to handle whatever comes my way.

What do I study?

Back home in South Africa, I'm studying at Belgium Campus, towards a Bachelor of Computing degree, with my main focus being Data Science. What drew me into this filed is how data can be used to understand patterns, solve problems, and even predict outcomes.

What do I like / hobbies?

When I'm not studying, I'm all about keeping life relaxed and fun. I spend a lot of time on the farm and really enjoy being outdoors. I get a kick out of exploring new places and seeing how people live differently, it's always fun to try things I've never done before. I'm also into gaming, reading, and binge-watching TV shows when I need to relax. On the more hands-on side, I enjoy restoring old cars with my father and brothers. I also like being active, whether it's hiking, swimming, or heading out on a spontaneous adventure.

What do I expect of the minor?

From this minor, I hope to gain not only academic knowledge but also practical experience working in an international environment. Since this is my first time overseas, I'm especially excited to explore a new country, experience different cultures, and see how people approach life and work in other parts of the world. I want to improve my teamwork skills, adaptability, and problem-solving abilities in cross-cultural settings. I also hope to build a network with fellow students and professionals, challenge myself by stepping outside of my comfort zone, and, fingers crossed, come out of it with a working prototype by the end of the program.

Work Philosophy

I try to keep it things straightforward, figuring them out as I go, working well with the people around me and taking responsibility for what I do. I like tackling challenges head-on. I see mistakes as chances to learn rather than setbacks. Being curious and adaptable helps me learn and handle challenges, while teamwork lets me combine ideas and get better results. At the end of the day, I want to work on projects that challenge me, let me grow, and leave me feeling like I actually made a difference.

Resume Overview

I have developed skills in programming, web development, data analysis, and problem-solving. My experience includes projects such as developing a POS system and a General Management System with role-based access, designing a hydroponics automation system using an ESP32, and working on various web development and IoT projects. These experiences have taught me how to collaborate with others, approach problems creatively, and stay adaptable while learning from both successes and mistakes.
